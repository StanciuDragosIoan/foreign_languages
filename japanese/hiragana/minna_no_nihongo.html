Affirmative: N1 は N2 です (N1 is N2)

N1 is the subject/topic of the sentence

は (HA but it is read WA) is the topic marking particle

N2 describes N1

です is a Japanese copula which indicates that N2 is the predicate, it describes N1 and also shows respect to the listener

*Note casual speech will transform です into だ

*Note there is also a 'most formal' way of for this structure namely: でございます

Examples:

I am Rose.

わたしはロ—ズ です。

I am a Filipino.

わたしはフィリピンじん です。

*Note the じん suffix is used to show nationality

Mr. Dela Cruz is a teacher.

デラクルズ さん せんせい です。

*note when talking about 'others', Japanese people use せんせい, however out of courtesy when speaking about oneself, they modestly say
わたし は きょうし です。

Negative: N1 は N2 じゃ ありません (N1 is not N2)

*Note formal negation is N1 は N2 では ありません

I am not Maria.

わたし は マリーア じゃ ありません。

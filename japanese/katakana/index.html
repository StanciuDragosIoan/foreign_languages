Katakana Japanese Writing System

There are 3 alphabetes in Japanese: Hiragana, Katakana and Kanji.

Katakana is the exact equivalent of Hiragana when it comes to pronounciation. The main difference is how the characters look and when they are used. They look completely different from Hiragana and are more angular. Only a few characters look similar (like か -hiragana / カ -katakana)

The Katakana script is used for foreign/loan words in order for natives to get the pronounciation. They are also used for sound effects (in manga).

It is also use for scientifical terms and for emphasis (just like English uses italics).
